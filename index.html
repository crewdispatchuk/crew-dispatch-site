Who we are

We are running a Southampton pilot for a short notice shift system that connects local workers with nearby venues when they need extra support during busy periods.

The work is simple floor support in busy bars, including collecting glasses, clearing tables and keeping areas tidy.

Shifts are a minimum of two hours and paid at least minimum wage, with venues often offering higher pay for urgent support.

All shifts are paid directly by the venue at the end of the shift and will always be located within a maximum 20 minute walk from your postcode.

How the trial works

  • The pilot will be on Zoom - you will be logged into a Zoom call from 8pm to 11pm while at home or nearby. Your microphone and camera do not need to be on.
  • You can be doing your own thing, but you must stay logged in, responsive and ready to leave if you accept a shift.
  • You are always free to accept or ignore shift requests — you are in control of whether you take a shift.
  • You must be dressed in standard black bar shift clothing and ready to leave your home immediately if you accept a shift.

How shifts are assigned

When a venue requests support, a message will be posted in the Zoom chat.

The first person to reply “YES – READY” gets the shift.

Prize draw

All registrations will be entered into a £50 cash draw.

Crew members who attend the full trial evenings (remaining logged in and responsive for both trial shifts) will be entered into an additional £100 prize draw.

Winners will be announced after the pilot concludes.

We need a certain number of registrations to start the pilot - You will receive the pilot date and Zoom invite by email.
